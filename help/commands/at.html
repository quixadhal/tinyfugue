/at

Usage:

/REQUIRE at.tf

/AT when commands

Commands will be executed at time-of-day when, which must be of the form "hours:minutes" or "hours:minutes:seconds" (hours is between 0 and 23, and seconds may be specified to the nearest microsecond). If when is less than equal to the current time of day, it is interpreted as that time tomorrow. The "equal" condition makes it possible for /at to call /at again with the same when argument, and have it execute tomorrow instead of immediately.

Example: /at 23:30 /echo Stop mudding, it's time for Letterman!

See: processes, /repeat, /quote
